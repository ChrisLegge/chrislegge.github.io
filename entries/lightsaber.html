Custom Lightsaber

Feburary 2025 – March 2025

Overview

I built a fully functional Lightsaber using an Arduino mircrocontroller and WS2812-b LEDs that can be indivdually addessed to create animations through my code. I added switches and buttons for controls and used professional workshop equiptment to create the handle.

The Handle

The handle is mainly made from 2 tubes of PVC pipe, one that fit completely inside the other. After making a design, I cut the PVC with a hacksaw and used a belt sander to get the pieces smooth. For the parts with details or with bevels I used the laithe to cut into the PVC evenly to get the right details. For the grip strips I used a laser cutter and a sheet of 3mm acrylic to get an accurate shape, I then used a polymer welding glue to put all the pieces together after spray painting them the right colour, with added details such as dirt to add wear and tear. I designed a small circle to cover the bottom so there would be no hole at the bottom of the PVC pipe.

The Blade

The Blade was made from a spare blade that I bought cheap from a lightsaber shop, I then added 2 strips of WS2812-b LEDs that I soldered end to end so that were part of the same strip. I stuck the 2 strips back to back with a wooden pole through the middle so it diddn't collapse in the middle. I covered the LEDs in packing foam as a cheap diffuser and connected the 3 pins to a removable connector that I soldered the other end to an Arduino, I also added a battery, a switch and a button. After some programming and a lot of testing I could put all the electronics in the lightsaber. I had a switch that cut power to the battery on the back, along with a removable panel to change the battery. An on and off switch that was programmed into the Arduino so that the blade would do an animation to slide up or down the blade, and a button to change the colour. I am going to add other animations, which I have already programmed, for when I press and hold the button.
